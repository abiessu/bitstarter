FAQ

What makes an engine a rotary engine?
Most engines supply rotational motion. The typical meaning of rotary is that all power-generating components are arranged rotationally in a common plane about the drive shaft axis.
What separates a vane engine from a piston engine?
A vane engine has rotationally-acting power-generating components (called vanes) whose naturally rotational motion is transferred to the drive shaft almost directly. A piston engine has linearly-acting power-generating components which have reciprocating motion which must be transformed by some means into rotational motion prior to transfer to the drive shaft.
